Mbeoh Royal Dynasty

The founders of this chiefdom came from Atoh(Ataw) in Dschang led by Kwewung towards the end of the 18th century. After a brief stay at Atel- Andeng kwewung then moved into the forest zone with his followers and founded his chiefdom along the upper valley of Ntse- Mbeoh (river). He put up his palace at Afouh in Lewoh Fondom

It is to be noted that Mbeoh is among the autonomous villages of the German map of December 1922 by Gadman. After the Germans Fontem Asonganyi started making moves for Fombeoh Atabonglefac to come to him. At his first invitation to Azi he was welcome with 03 women. Mami Ajongako was for him, the junior sister of Asaha Fontem Defang was for Forsah and Anumbu for Johnny Esehmoh.Fombeoh. and the members of his delegation accepted to be "Fonteh"in Lebang under no condition. The relationship was further consolidated when he was appointed judge in Fontem Customary court by the English administrator and Mbi Nwofaw Nyiawung, junior brother of Fontem Asonganyi, the underground mediator gave his own daughter, Bedulaboh to Fombeoh.Atabonglefac who is the mother of Fombeoh Ndemalia.

About

Mbeoh Cultural and Development Association Canada International (MBECUDA) is committed to empowering the sons and daughters of MBEOH and its environs. Our mission is to provide them with tools, support, and opportunities they need to thrive socially, economically,and psychologically through cultural enrichment, training, networking,and community engagement, with aim to facilitate their successful integration into Canadian society.

Mission

MBECUDA is committed to empowering the sons and daughters of the Mbeoh area in Cameroon who have made Calgary and Lethbridge their home. Our mission is to provide them with the tools, support, and opportunities they need to thrive socially, economically, and psychologically. Through cultural enrichment, training, networking, and community engagement, we aim to facilitate their successful integration into Canadian society, ultimately contributing to the alleviation of poverty within our community.

Vision

Our vision is a united and prosperous community of Mbeoh natives in Calgary, canada at large and other countries, fully integrated into Canadian society while preserving and celebrating our rich cultural heritage. We envision a community where every member has access to educational and economic opportunities, enjoys social well-being, and actively contributes to the growth and development of both their homeland and their adopted country.

Social Empowerment

To foster social empowerment by creating a supportive network that enhances the well-being and sense of belonging of Mbeoh natives in Calgary, Canada at large and other countries .

Economic Empowerment

To promote economic self-sufficiency and financial stability through training, mentorship, and opportunities for entrepreneurship or career advancement.

Cultural Preservation

To preserve, promote, and celebrate the rich cultural heritage of the Mbeoh area through cultural events, awareness campaigns, and educational programs.

Education and Training

To offer educational and training programs that empower community members with the knowledge and skills necessary for personal and professional development.

Networking and Collaboration

To foster collaboration and networking opportunities among Mbeoh natives, local organizations, and the broader Canadian community for mutual benefit and growth.

Psychological Support

To provide psychological support and resources to address the emotional and mental well-being of our community members, helping them navigate the challenges of immigration and integration.

Community Development

To actively contribute to the development of both the Mbeoh area in Cameroon and the local Canadian communities in Calgary and Canada at large through philanthropic initiatives and community-building efforts.

Advocacy

To advocate for the rights and interests of Mbeoh natives within the Canadian context and to champion causes that promote social justice, equity, and inclusivity. MBECUDA Canada International is dedicated to fulfilling these mandates to empower, uplift, and unite the sons and daughters of the Mbeoh area, fostering a brighter future both here in Canada and in their homeland

Executed and Ongoing Projects

  • MBECUCA mobilised resources and financially supported the construction of the Lebang road
  • MBECUCA through her benefactors and donors, contributed to the cleaning of the Mbeoh palace, hospital and hall. This has been left unattended due to the ongoing crisis in the North West and South West Regions of Cameroon
  • MBECUCA keeps providing aid andassistance to the internally displaced inhabitants of the Mbeoh community and the English Speaking Cameroonians in general
  • MBECUCA is currently working and mobilizing resources to support the solar energy project of the Lebang community

    His Royal Highness Fombeoh Ndemalia,the chief of Mbeoh

    He succeeded his father - Fombeoh Atabonglefac in 1964 and has since ruled Mbeoh till present day.

    "

    Fombeoh Atabonglefac made his exit on 13th December 1964 and Fombeoh Ndemalia took over the Mbeoh thrown after a long period of Regency of Mbe Keah Linus who was later on replayed for a short period by Pa Johnny Esehmoh. He was brought to the Fon of Fontem by the Fon of Foto Dschang who raised him. He was installed the same day - 18th April 1975.The fon of Fontem thanked the fon of Foto in his speech then presented the “new Fombeoh” to the public amidst thunderous applauses. He was let to his seat with Mafua and Nkweta by the late chiefs’friends, Bekem Mbeoh and family members. This was followed by jubilation and merry making. It was a great day for all the Mbeoh who came from far and near for that Historic Day. (Asaha had died at his very young age).
